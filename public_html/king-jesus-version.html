The Earthly King Has Passed

King James I of England, who commissioned the 1611 Authorized Version, died four hundred and one years ago. His tomb is in Westminster Abbey. His crown was set down. His scepter was buried with him. His version of the throne is gone.

But the Word he commissioned did not die with him. Because it was never really his.

"The grass withereth, the flower fadeth: but the word of our God shall stand for ever." — Isaiah 40:8

The True King Reigns

While King James decays in his tomb, the King whose Word fills those pages is alive forevermore:

"I am he that liveth, and was dead; and, behold, I am alive for evermore, Amen; and have the keys of hell and of death." — Revelation 1:18
"And he hath on his vesture and on his thigh a name written, KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS." — Revelation 19:16
